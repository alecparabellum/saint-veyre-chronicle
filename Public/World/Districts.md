# Districts.md

## Districts of Saint Veyre

Saint Veyre is divided into several major districts, each shaped by different centuries of development. While modern maps recognize administrative boundaries, residents often think of the city in terms of influence, reputation, and history.

Every district possesses its own identity, power structures, and social atmosphere.

A newcomer can learn much about a resident of Saint Veyre simply by learning where they spend their nights.

---

# Crown District

### "Power Wears Old Stone"

The Crown District occupies the historical and political center of Saint Veyre.

Grand avenues, government buildings, financial institutions, luxury residences, private clubs, and centuries-old estates dominate the district. Many of the city's wealthiest families maintain a presence here.

The architecture reflects several eras of prosperity, creating a mixture of medieval foundations, gothic facades, and modern renovations.

Visitors often describe the district as beautiful.

Residents often describe it as expensive.

### Notable Features

* Municipal Hall
* The Royal Exchange
* Numerous private clubs
* Historic mansions
* Financial institutions
* Luxury shopping streets

### Reputation

Among mortals, the Crown District represents prestige.

Among Kindred, it represents influence.

---

# Cathedral Hill

### "The City Remembers"

Built atop one of Saint Veyre's highest elevations, Cathedral Hill serves as the spiritual and historical heart of the city.

Ancient cathedrals, museums, archives, universities, libraries, and monuments fill the district. Narrow streets wind between centuries-old structures, creating a labyrinth of history overlooking the modern skyline.

The district attracts scholars, tourists, clergy, and historians.

Many residents claim Cathedral Hill feels older than the city itself.

### Notable Features

* Saint Veyre Cathedral
* National Historical Archives
* Saint Veyre University
* Museum Quarter
* Old Bell Tower
* Memorial Gardens

### Reputation

A district of memory, tradition, and secrets buried beneath centuries of stone.

---

# Iron Quarter

### "Where The City Fights Back"

The Iron Quarter was once the industrial heart of Saint Veyre.

Factories, foundries, rail infrastructure, and worker housing defined the district for generations. Though much of its industry has declined, the area remains fiercely independent.

Redevelopment projects have transformed portions of the district into nightlife centers, creative spaces, and residential developments.

The process remains controversial.

### Notable Features

* Converted factories
* Entertainment venues
* Warehouse districts
* Independent businesses
* Historic rail yards
* Industrial waterfront

### Reputation

The Iron Quarter distrusts authority.

Whether that reputation is deserved depends on who is asked.

---

# River Wards

### "Everything Arrives Through The River"

Stretching along both sides of the Greywash River, the River Wards form one of the most diverse areas of Saint Veyre.

The district serves as a gateway between the city and the outside world. Shipping, trade, immigration, logistics, and commerce have shaped the area's identity for centuries.

Cultures from across the world have left their mark here.

### Notable Features

* Commercial docks
* Shipping terminals
* Markets
* Freight infrastructure
* Waterfront neighborhoods
* Merchant districts

### Reputation

If information enters Saint Veyre from elsewhere, chances are it passes through the River Wards first.

---

# Glass Harbor

### "The Future Has Arrived"

Glass Harbor represents Saint Veyre's newest face.

Constructed through massive redevelopment projects over the last several decades, the district showcases modern architecture, luxury developments, corporate headquarters, and entertainment venues.

Towering glass structures dominate the skyline.

Supporters see progress.

Critics see displacement.

### Notable Features

* Corporate headquarters
* Luxury condominiums
* Financial technology firms
* Waterfront promenades
* High-end restaurants
* Entertainment complexes

### Reputation

A district built for ambition.

Few places in Saint Veyre generate wealth faster.

---

# Blackthorn

### "The Forgotten Ring"

Located beyond the city's historic core, Blackthorn consists of aging residential neighborhoods, declining commercial corridors, and pockets of stubborn local culture.

Many residents feel ignored by City Hall and overshadowed by redevelopment elsewhere.

Despite this, Blackthorn maintains a strong community identity.

### Notable Features

* Residential neighborhoods
* Local businesses
* Public housing complexes
* Community centers
* Older parks
* Historic cemeteries

### Reputation

Saint Veyre often forgets Blackthorn.

Blackthorn has not forgotten Saint Veyre.

---

# The Heights

### "Above The Noise"

The Heights occupies the elevated northern edge of the city.

Wealthy residential enclaves, gated estates, private schools, and panoramic views have made it one of the most desirable areas in Saint Veyre.

Privacy is highly valued here.

### Notable Features

* Luxury estates
* Private academies
* Exclusive residential communities
* Country clubs
* Botanical gardens
* Scenic overlooks

### Reputation

Many of Saint Veyre's most influential residents prefer to live where they can observe the city without being observed themselves.

---

# The Undertunnels

### "Beneath Saint Veyre"

Beneath the visible city lies an extensive network of forgotten infrastructure.

Abandoned transit routes, utility corridors, maintenance passages, storm drains, and historical foundations stretch beneath much of Saint Veyre.

Most citizens never think about what exists below them.

Many would prefer not to know.

### Notable Features

* Disused tunnels
* Maintenance networks
* Historic foundations
* Utility infrastructure
* Flood channels
* Sealed passages

### Reputation

The Undertunnels appear on few tourist maps.

Yet they connect much of Saint Veyre in ways the surface rarely understands.

---

# District Dynamics

While every district possesses its own character, several rivalries shape the city.

### Crown District vs Glass Harbor

Old wealth versus new wealth.

Tradition versus innovation.

Influence versus momentum.

---

### Cathedral Hill vs Redevelopment Interests

Preservation versus expansion.

History versus profitability.

---

### Iron Quarter vs City Hall

Independence versus oversight.

Local identity versus modernization.

---

### Blackthorn vs The Rest Of The City

Recognition versus neglect.

---

# Advice For New Arrivals

A common mistake among newcomers is believing Saint Veyre is one city.

Veterans know better.

Saint Veyre is many cities sharing the same skyline.

The Crown District speaks one language.

The Iron Quarter speaks another.

The River Wards speak dozens.

A Kindred seeking influence must eventually learn them all.

For in Saint Veyre, geography is rarely just geography.

Every street belongs to a story.

Every district belongs to someone.
