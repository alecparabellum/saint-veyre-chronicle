# Chronicle Recap Packet

## Session 1: First Night at the Iron Opera

Alec Le Veil spent her first weekend in Saint Veyre attending the Iron Opera after being instructed to formally introduce herself to the local Camarilla authorities.

Upon arrival, she met Lucien Armand, Keeper of Elysium, and Geneviève Laurent, leading Harpy of Saint Veyre.

Alec was invited to a more exclusive gathering where she formally introduced herself to Prince Augustus Voss. During the gathering, she discussed her ambitions, her reasons for coming to Saint Veyre, and her intention to contribute to the city rather than merely benefit from it.

She made favorable impressions upon:

- Prince Augustus Voss
- Lucien Armand
- Geneviève Laurent
- Gabriel Ortega
- Eleanor Whitmore

After the court gathering, Alec spoke privately with Geneviève. Their conversation touched upon Alec's strained relationship with her sire, Vivienne Rousseau, her conflicted feelings regarding the Embrace, and her hopes that Saint Veyre might offer a different future.

The evening concluded with Alec and Geneviève establishing a personal rapport and mutual attraction.

## Session 2: Studies in Absence

Several nights later, Eleanor Whitmore contacted Alec and requested an outline of Maison Le Veil's intended commercial presence in Saint Veyre.

Alec explained that she intended to begin by opening a Maison Le Veil fragrance store, while remaining open to future collaborations and expansion into adjacent luxury industries. Eleanor accepted Alec's invitation to discuss the plan further and arranged a meeting at Whitmore House for the following night.

Geneviève invited Alec to a private exhibition called *Studies in Absence* at Atelier Seventeen.

Before attending, Alec purchased a dark green Aston Martin DB12 and used the drive to explore Saint Veyre.

At the exhibition, Alec met its host, Camille Vauclair.

A confrontation developed around an artwork titled *The Missing Heir*. A dark-skinned woman named Marianne Saint-Fleur objected to the artwork's depiction of racial exclusion and to the curator's dismissive treatment of her concerns.

Alec approached Marianne openly and validated her discomfort. She privately introduced herself by her birth name, Alexandra Jean, as a gesture of trust and solidarity.

Camille and Geneviève joined the discussion. The four women moved into a private preparation room, where Camille acknowledged that she had underestimated what the artwork demanded from Black viewers.

Marianne requested:

- A revised artist's statement
- A revised gallery note explicitly addressing racial exclusion
- The removal of the curator from guest-facing duties

Alec helped negotiate a resolution that respected Marianne's concerns without turning the incident into a public spectacle attached to her name.

Camille accepted the first two requests and removed the curator from the exhibition floor for the rest of the evening. She also agreed to review his future involvement.

The four women returned to the exhibition together.

By the end of the evening:

- Alec had earned Marianne's respect.
- Camille regarded Alec with increased interest and consideration.
- Geneviève's personal interest in Alec had deepened.
- Alec's emerging reputation had expanded beyond ambition and elegance to include courage, loyalty, and mediation.
