# Portrait Variation Matrix

Use this matrix before generating a new definitive or alternate character portrait. Select one option from each category, then compare the combination against recent major-character images.

## Crop

- Tight bust.
- Chest-up.
- Waist-up.
- Half-body.
- Half-body with visible hands.

## Camera Height

- Slightly below eye level.
- Eye level.
- Slightly above eye level.
- Seated-eye level.

## Body Orientation

- Frontal.
- Three-quarter left.
- Three-quarter right.
- Shoulders turned away, face returned toward viewer.
- Profile-adjacent without full profile.

## Gaze

- Direct eye contact.
- Looking just past viewer.
- Looking toward an off-frame person.
- Downward attention to an object.
- Upward or distant attention.

## Hands

- Outside frame.
- One hand resting on furniture.
- Hands loosely joined.
- One hand holding a meaningful object.
- One hand adjusting clothing or accessory.
- Gesture interrupted mid-action.

## Negative Space

- Balanced around subject.
- Expanded to the left.
- Expanded to the right.
- Compressed by architecture.
- Open above the subject.

## Light Direction

- Window side-light.
- Warm practical light from below eye line.
- Cool backlight with soft facial fill.
- Split light from two eras or technologies.
- Reflected city light.
- Overhead architectural light softened by environment.

## Background Depth

- Shallow, recognizable shapes.
- Medium depth with one readable landmark.
- Deep corridor or room perspective.
- Layered glass or reflection.
- Foreground obstruction with softened location beyond.

## Emotional Temperature

- Controlled warmth.
- Appraising neutrality.
- Guarded amusement.
- Quiet fatigue.
- Contained hostility.
- Private uncertainty.
- Ceremonial authority.
- Predatory stillness.

## Foreground Device

- None.
- Doorframe or curtain edge.
- Reflective glass.
- Furniture corner.
- Out-of-focus document or object.
- Architectural railing or balustrade.

## Variation Rule

No two consecutive major-character portraits should repeat the same crop, body orientation, gaze, light direction, and emotional temperature combination.

## Anti-Template Check

Reject or redesign the concept when three or more of these match a recently approved portrait:

- Crop.
- Camera height.
- Body orientation.
- Gaze.
- Hand treatment.
- Light direction.
- Background type.
- Emotional temperature.
- Foreground device.
