## Identity Anchors

- **Apparent age:** Late thirties.
- **Ancestry:** Indian.
- **Height impression:** Average to slightly tall.
- **Body type:** Slender, lean, and narrow-framed.
- **Proportions:** Long limbs, angular shoulders, and a restrained physical silhouette.
- **Skin tone:** Deep brown with warm-to-neutral undertones.
- **Face shape:** Long oval face.
- **Cheekbones:** High and prominent without becoming sharply hollow.
- **Jaw and chin:** Narrow jaw with a softly pointed chin.
- **Nose:** Long, substantial, and clearly defined, with natural asymmetry.
- **Eyes:** Large, dark brown, expressive, and deeply observant.
- **Eye setting:** Slightly deep-set with visible under-eye shadows and hollows.
- **Eyebrows:** Thick, dark, mostly straight, and naturally shaped.
- **Lips:** Medium-full, restrained, and naturally asymmetrical.
- **Skin characteristics:** Visible pores, fine lines, subtle pigmentation variation, under-eye texture, and a lived-in professional appearance.
- **Hair colour:** Black.
- **Hair texture:** Straight to softly wavy.
- **Hair length:** Long.
- **Canonical working hairstyle:** Low ponytail or practical loose knot with escaped strands around the temples and cheeks.
- **Defining accessories:** Small silver or dark-metal stud earrings.
- **Signature presence:** Calm professional scrutiny with an undercurrent of sleepless unease.

## Palette

- **Dominant colours:** Charcoal, black, smoke grey, dark umber.
- **Secondary colours:** Aged paper, muted steel, old brass, brown glass.
- **Accent colour:** Dull amber from practical task lighting.
- **Kindred accent:** Pale reflective ivory or muted amber within the eyes.
- **Preferred neutrals:** Ash, bone, desaturated brown, and stainless steel.
- **Associated materials:** Worn cotton, practical wool, brushed steel, aged paper, cloudy glass, old wood, surgical metal, and preserved biological specimens.
- **Skin-lighting preference:** Warm desk or examination light balanced against cool laboratory ambience.
- **Colours to avoid:** Bright jewel tones, glamorous red-and-black palettes, saturated medical blue, decorative purple, or clan-coded colour schemes.
- **Conditions that justify a palette shift:** Kindred imagery may become colder, more desaturated, and faintly green-grey while preserving her deep natural skin tone.

## Composition Grammar

- **Preferred crop:** Waist-up, half-body, working portrait, and close facial portrait.
- **Camera height:** Eye level.
- **Body orientation:** Frontal or restrained three-quarter position.
- **Gaze:** Direct examination, attention focused upon evidence, or a glance directed toward something beyond the visible scene.
- **Symmetry:** Controlled asymmetry.
- **Negative space:** Shelving, instruments, specimen jars, windows, and examination tables may create quiet spatial pressure.
- **Foreground layering:** Medical trays, folders, glass containers, notes, or lamps may partially frame the image.
- **Background depth:** Recognizable but softened forensic office, mortuary, laboratory, archive, or medical research room.
- **Lens impression:** Approximately 65–100mm for definitive portraits; 50–70mm for working environments.
- **Degree of movement:** Minimal. Her hands may work while the rest of her remains unusually still.

## Posture and Gesture

- **How she occupies space:** Quietly and efficiently.
- **Shoulders:** Slightly narrow and relaxed.
- **Spine:** Upright without formality.
- **Head position:** Level or subtly tilted while considering evidence.
- **Typical hand behaviour:** Writing notes, adjusting instruments, holding a file, touching a specimen container, or resting gloved fingers beside evidence.
- **Distance from viewer:** Close enough to feel observed.
- **Characteristic expressions:** Focused attention, intellectual curiosity, restrained concern, dry amusement, clinical neutrality, and disquieting recognition.
- **Expressions to avoid:** Broad glamour smiles, theatrical insanity, exaggerated fear, manic laughter, seductive posing, or cartoonish menace.
- **Physical tension:** Very low. Unease should arise from her composure rather than visible agitation.

## Public Persona

Asha presents herself as a disciplined medical professional.

She does not perform eccentricity and does not use her unsettling reputation as theatre.

Her credibility depends upon accuracy, calm, and the impression that she has already considered explanations other people have not yet dared to articulate.

Her beauty should remain realistic, mature, and individual.

She is memorable because of her face, attention, and quiet fatigue rather than cosmetic perfection.

---

## Shadow Self

Asha's visual contradiction lies between clinical certainty and uncertain perception.

She appears to be the most rational person in the room.

The viewer gradually suspects that she is also perceiving something no one else can see.

Her attention may shift toward an empty corner, a reflection, a corpse, or a specimen before any visible explanation emerges.

The unease should never clarify whether she is experiencing revelation, hallucination, supernatural insight, or all three at once.

---

## Supernatural Expression

### Routine Images

Permitted subtle signs:

- Her gaze remains fixed slightly too long.
- Her eyes catch light from an implausible direction.
- Her skin appears cool beneath otherwise warm illumination.
- Background reflections seem misaligned with her attention.
- She notices a detail before the viewer can identify it.
- A specimen, body, or document appears visually ordinary while her expression implies otherwise.
- She remains perfectly still while loose hair, paper, or curtains move.
- Her eyes seem focused both upon the immediate subject and something farther away.

### Fifth Standard Image: Kindred Vision

The fifth image should reveal Asha's vampiric and Malkavian nature more clearly without turning her into a theatrical monster.

Permitted overt signs:

- Eyes becoming pale, reflective, or faintly amber-white.
- Pupils appearing difficult to locate.
- Skin losing warmth and becoming subtly corpse-cool.
- Fangs visible only if her lips part naturally.
- Her attention directed toward something absent from the visible room.
- Reflections or peripheral anatomy appearing slightly inconsistent.
- A corpse or preserved specimen forming a calm professional context rather than a gore display.
- The environment appearing temporally still, as though the room has paused around her.
- A slight mismatch between her reflection, shadow, and physical orientation.
- The sense that she has recognized a pattern within death that the viewer cannot perceive.

Avoid exaggerated glowing eyes, screaming, blood-soaked frenzy, or asylum-horror caricature.

## Exclusions

- **Incorrect face:** Round glamour face, tiny nose, sharply hollow cheeks, oversized eyes, perfect symmetry, or youthful beauty-standard smoothing.
- **Incorrect skin:** Pale, light brown, greyed, or heavily desaturated skin that erases her Indian complexion.
- **Incorrect hair:** Short crop, ornate curls, elaborate braids, fashion updo, or perfectly polished high ponytail.
- **Incorrect wardrobe:** Evening gowns, luxury suits, clan-coded costumes, corsets, excessive jewellery, or glamorous mortician styling.
- **Incorrect personality cues:** Manic expressions, wild gesticulation, constant fear, helplessness, seductive posing, or theatrical cruelty.
- **Incorrect supernatural effects:** Neon eyes, floating symbols, cracked porcelain skin, shadow tentacles, animal companions, or generic psychic energy.
- **Cultural errors:** Decorative bindis, ceremonial jewellery, sari-inspired costume, or religious symbolism added solely to signal Indian ancestry.
- **Cross-character contamination:** Geneviève Laurent's plum elegance, Marianne Saint-Fleur's tattoos and crows, Geneviève D'Artois's monumental black velvet, Eleanor Whitmore's silver bob, or Naw Thiri's activist streetwear.

## Approved Variation Range

- **Hair variation:** Low ponytail, loose practical knot, partially released hair, or hair tucked behind one ear. Length and black colour remain fixed.
- **Wardrobe variation:** Dark medical coat, charcoal jacket, black shirt, practical trousers, restrained knitwear, or simple institutional attire.
- **Makeup variation:** Minimal or none. Slight under-eye definition may remain natural rather than cosmetic.
- **Environment flexibility:** Forensic, medical, academic, archival, investigative, and institutional environments throughout Saint Veyre.
- **Palette flexibility:** Charcoal, black, smoke, old paper, steel, umber, and muted amber.
- **Supernatural escalation:** Reflective eyes, cold skin, fangs, misaligned reflection, and perceptual distortion are reserved for the fifth image or dramatic story moments.

## Standard Five-Image Set

1. **Forensic office portrait**
   - Waist-up or half-body.
   - Dark professional clothing.
   - Case files, practical light, and old institutional architecture.

2. **Research desk portrait**
   - Seated while writing or reconstructing evidence.
   - Medical notes, diagrams, and restrained anatomical materials.
   - Calm direct gaze.

3. **Mortuary portrait**
   - Standing beside an examination table or surgical instruments.
   - Cold task lighting.
   - Professional control rather than horror spectacle.

4. **Definitive close portrait**
   - Long oval face, substantial nose, expressive eyes, under-eye shadows, and natural skin texture clearly visible.
   - Warm background light against a dark professional collar.

5. **Kindred vision portrait**
   - Pale reflective eyes or difficult-to-locate pupils.
   - Subtle corpse-cool skin.
   - A body or preserved specimen in the environment.
   - Perceptual wrongness rather than frenzy.
   - She remains calm, lucid, and frighteningly attentive.
