## Identity Anchors

- **Apparent age:** Early thirties.
- **Height impression:** Above average, elegant, and long-limbed.
- **Body type and proportions:** Slender with graceful shoulders, a long neck, and elongated proportions. She should appear healthy and physically present rather than fragile or extremely thin.
- **Skin tone and undertones:** Warm medium-brown skin with neutral-to-golden undertones.
- **Face shape:** Distinctly long oval face with a high forehead, prominent cheekbones, a narrow jaw, and a gently tapered chin.
- **Facial structure:** Refined but not generically symmetrical. Her bone structure should remain angular enough to be memorable while retaining softness around the mouth and eyes.
- **Nose:** Strong and prominent, with a defined bridge and substantial natural shape. Do not reduce it to a small conventional beauty-standard nose.
- **Eyes:** Dark brown, almond-shaped, moderately sized, and slightly heavy-lidded. Her eyes should appear observant rather than permanently seductive.
- **Eye spacing:** Natural-to-slightly wide spacing that supports her long facial proportions.
- **Eyebrows:** Thick, dark, carefully shaped, and expressive.
- **Lips:** Full and sculpted, with a defined upper lip and naturally asymmetrical detail.
- **Ears:** Natural proportions, usually partially visible through or beneath her hairstyle.
- **Skin characteristics:** Visible natural texture, pores, faint tonal variation, and a small beauty mark high upon one cheek.
- **Teeth:** Natural adult teeth. They should not be rendered as unnaturally uniform cosmetic veneers.
- **Hair texture:** Naturally curly.
- **Hair colour:** Very dark brown, nearly black.
- **Hair length:** Medium-to-long when released.
- **Established hairstyle or silhouette:** Elegant softly structured updo with selected curls framing the face. Loose or partially loose curls are acceptable in private scenes.
- **Distinguishing marks:** Small beauty mark upon one cheek.
- **Signature styling:** Controlled dark luxury, elongated jewellery, deep plum accents, and impeccable but unshowy presentation.
- **Defining accessories:** Antique-style drop earrings and necklaces set with dark purple gemstones.

## Palette

- **Dominant colours:** Black, deep plum, aubergine, dark charcoal.
- **Secondary colours:** Muted wine, antique bronze, warm brown, smoke.
- **Accent colour:** Dark amethyst.
- **Preferred neutrals:** Blackened velvet, charcoal, warm ivory used sparingly.
- **Associated materials:** Velvet, silk, satin, carved dark wood, aged gold, polished stone, smoked glass.
- **Skin-lighting preference:** Warm amber key light with restrained cool architectural fill. Her skin should remain rich and dimensional rather than lightened or greyed.
- **Colours to avoid:** Bright scarlet as a default, bubblegum pink, icy blue glamour palettes, or universal Toreador rose tones.
- **Conditions that justify a palette shift:** Public ceremonies may introduce restrained gold; intimate scenes may use warmer brown and burgundy; threatening scenes may move toward near-black plum and cold reflected glass.

---

## Composition Grammar

- **Preferred crop:** Bust, chest-up, waist-up, and half-body.
- **Camera height:** Eye level or slightly below, without excessive heroic distortion.
- **Body orientation:** Three-quarter orientation, elegant profile, or a composed frontal position.
- **Gaze:** Direct but measured, directed toward an off-frame speaker, or turned back toward the viewer after first observing someone else.
- **Symmetry or asymmetry:** Controlled asymmetry. The composition may initially feel formal but should contain one destabilizing detail.
- **Negative space:** Clean and deliberate, often shaped by architecture, curtains, mirrors, or blurred social figures.
- **Foreground layering:** Doorframes, glass, a shoulder in soft focus, curtains, or architectural rails may imply that Geneviève is observing or being observed.
- **Background depth:** Recognizable but softened salons, Elysium chambers, galleries, opera interiors, private reception rooms, and high windows overlooking Saint Veyre.
- **Lens impression:** Approximately 70–105mm for definitive portraits and 50–70mm for environmental half-body images.
- **Degree of stillness or motion:** Geneviève herself remains composed while guests, reflections, fabric, or candlelight provide subtle movement.

---

## Posture and Gesture

- **How the character occupies space:** She never appears to ask permission for space, but she does not seize it theatrically.
- **Shoulder and spine posture:** Upright spine, relaxed shoulders, long neck, and slightly elevated chin.
- **Typical hand behaviour:** Adjusting a cuff, lightly touching jewellery, resting upon furniture, holding a glass without drinking, or making restrained conversational gestures.
- **Distance from viewer:** Intimate enough for her appraisal to feel personally directed.
- **Characteristic expressions:** Quiet assessment, restrained amusement, attentive warmth, polite skepticism, subtle disappointment, and private curiosity.
- **Expressions to avoid:** Broad theatrical sneers, exaggerated seduction, vacant glamour, cartoonish cruelty, constant smirking, or overt rage in routine portraits.
- **Degree and type of physical tension:** Minimal visible tension. When displeased, tension should emerge around the eyes, mouth, jaw, or fingers rather than through aggressive posture.

## Environment

- **Preferred portrait locations:** Elysium receptions, private salons, galleries, opera boxes, converted industrial cultural spaces, refined offices, and rooms overlooking Saint Veyre.
- **Relevant district or social world:** Camarilla court, cultural institutions, private social circles, and spaces where reputation is created.
- **Background architecture:** Gothic arches, carved wood, historic stone, contemporary glass, converted industrial steelwork, velvet curtains, and warm chandeliers.
- **Recurring environmental materials:** Velvet, marble, dark wood, brass, smoked glass, silk, and polished metal.
- **Meaningful props:** Seating arrangements, invitations, correspondence, guest lists, mirrors, opera programmes, understated jewellery, and carefully positioned glasses.
- **Status markers:** Other people subtly orienting themselves toward her, access to private rooms, reserved seating, social deference, and calm ownership of prestigious spaces.
- **Props or symbols to avoid overusing:** Roses, rose petals, crowns, fans, masks, blood-filled glasses, peacocks, generic art palettes, or obvious Toreador logos.

## Public Persona

Geneviève intends to appear attentive, cultivated, fair, and socially effortless.

Her presentation communicates that she has nothing to prove and no need to rush. She gives others enough warmth to reveal themselves while maintaining sufficient distance to judge what they reveal.

Her beauty is important, but it should never become her only visual idea.

The lasting impression should be intelligence wearing elegance rather than elegance pretending to be intelligence.

---

## Shadow Self

Geneviève's composure conceals constant evaluation.

Her visual contradiction lies between warmth and measurement. She may appear relaxed while her gaze tracks a conversation elsewhere, or smile gently while a reflection reveals that her attention has shifted.

The room may be socially crowded, yet she should appear psychologically separate from it.

---

## Supernatural Expression

### Routine Images

Permitted subtle signs:

- Unnaturally complete stillness.
- An eye reflection that does not match the nearest light.
- A mirror composition that feels slightly delayed or emotionally inconsistent.
- Other guests becoming less distinct while her attention remains unnaturally sharp.
- Skin that appears cool only in the deepest shadow.
- A gaze that feels too sustained without becoming visibly monstrous.

### Dramatic Images

Overt signs permitted only when narratively justified:

- Exposed fangs.
- Predatory fixation that replaces her usual social warmth.
- Beauty intensified into something disquieting rather than physically transformed.
- Reflections or surrounding faces distorted by supernatural presence.
- Heightened colour contrast around the eyes and mouth.
- A room becoming unnaturally silent or visually arrested around her.

Routine portraits should not use glowing eyes, blood tears, animal swarms, or monstrous facial transformations.

## Continuity Rules

Preserve unless explicitly changed:

- Long oval facial shape.
- High forehead.
- Prominent cheekbones.
- Narrow jaw and tapered chin.
- Strong prominent nose.
- Warm medium-brown skin.
- Dark brown almond-shaped eyes.
- Thick dark eyebrows.
- Full sculpted lips.
- Small cheek beauty mark.
- Natural skin texture.
- Very dark curly hair.
- Slender long-limbed build.
- Deep plum, black, and amethyst visual family.
- Antique-style purple gemstone jewellery.
- Controlled, economical posture and gestures.

Additional character-specific continuity rules:

- Do not restore the former auburn hair description.
- Do not lighten her skin between images.
- Do not shorten or conventionalize her nose.
- Do not widen her jaw into a generic heart-shaped glamour face.
- Do not enlarge her eyes into a doll-like proportion.
- Do not smooth away her beauty mark or all skin texture.
- Her facial identity should remain recognizable without relying upon jewellery or clothing.
- Her beauty must remain distinctive rather than standardized.

---

## Exclusions

- **Incorrect features:** Auburn, red, blonde, or straight hair; pale skin; blue or green eyes; small upturned nose; round face; short broad facial proportions; thin eyebrows; oversized eyes; porcelain-smooth skin.
- **Misleading poses or expressions:** Exaggerated seduction, constant direct confrontation, theatrical disdain, broad villainous smiles, or submissive glamour posing.
- **Generic visual clichés:** Roses, rose petals, theatrical masks, blood goblets, permanent red lighting, rococo overload, or fashion-editorial vacancy.
- **Inappropriate locations:** Generic medieval throne rooms, bedrooms used primarily for seduction, random cathedrals without social context, or wilderness settings.
- **Wardrobe errors:** Costume-like period gowns as a default, excessive lace, bright clan-coded red, overloaded jewellery, or clothing that looks chosen solely to expose skin.
- **Cross-character contamination:** Crows, tribal tattoos, face paint, muscular streetwear styling, gold hoops, or other visual anchors belonging to Marianne Saint-Fleur.

---

## Approved Variation Range

- **Hair changes permitted:** Formal updo, loosely pinned curls, half-up styling, or fully loose curls. Texture and very dark colour remain fixed.
- **Wardrobe changes permitted:** Tailored suits, evening dresses, velvet jackets, silk blouses, refined coats, and elegant contemporary separates.
- **Makeup or grooming changes permitted:** Restrained variation in plum, brown, neutral, or wine tones. Her natural facial structure and skin texture must remain visible.
- **Palette flexibility:** Black, plum, aubergine, wine, charcoal, bronze, and amethyst may vary in proportion.
- **Environment flexibility:** Court, culture, luxury, and private social environments throughout Saint Veyre.
- **Supernatural escalation boundaries:** Overt fangs or predatory distortion only during feeding, confrontation, Discipline use, or a major dramatic reveal.
